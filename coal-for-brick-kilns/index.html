What kilns buy, and why

The firing fuel of choice is hard coke — its dense, high-carbon lumps hold the sustained, even heat a brick firing needs, with far less smoke than raw coal. Kilns choose between chota (small) and bada (large) grades based on how they pack and feed the fuel; both are stocked at our depot and open for inspection.

Alongside lump coke, many kilns take hard coke dust — coke breeze — the fine grade that costs less per tonne. Blended sensibly, it brings down the fuel cost per thousand bricks, which is why cost-conscious operators order lump and dust together.
