Coal Supply for Tea Gardens in Assam

Assam's tea gardens are heat-hungry operations, and coal remains a practical fuel for garden and factory heating needs. Our depot sits on NH 37 in Guwahati — the natural dispatch point for truck loads into the tea districts — and tea gardens are among the customers we serve.

Quick answer

Tea gardens and their factories in Assam buy coal primarily as a heating fuel, with steam coal (kacha koyla) as the economical bulk option and hard coke where steadier, low-smoke heat is needed. Anant Coal Depot in Guwahati (NH 37, Lalmati, est. 1990) supplies both by truck load across Assam's tea districts, with GST billing and market-linked rates quoted on enquiry at 94350 18204 — share your garden's location and seasonal requirement for a delivered quote.

What tea operations order

For general and factory heating needs, the workhorse is steam coal — kacha koyla — bought by the tonne or truck load as the economical bulk fuel. Where an application calls for steadier, cleaner-burning heat, gardens take hard coke instead; the trade-offs between the two are laid out in our comparison guide. Garden workshops and canteens also pick up wood charcoal in smaller quantities.
