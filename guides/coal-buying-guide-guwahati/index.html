Step 1: Pick the right product

"Coal" covers several different products, and buying the wrong one wastes money. In short: hard coke for furnaces, bhattis and kilns; steam coal (kacha koyla) for boilers and general heating; wood charcoal (lakdi koyla) for tandoors, grills and home use; hard coke dust as an economical blending fuel; and earthing coal and earthing salt for electrical earthing pits, which are not fuels at all. If you are torn between the first two, read our hard coke vs steam coal comparison.

Step 2: Know the units the trade uses

Coal in India is quoted and sold by weight. The units you will hear are: kg for small retail purchases, quintal (100 kg) for mid-size orders, tonne (1,000 kg, i.e. 10 quintals) for bulk, and tempo or truck load for industrial supply. If you are unsure how these convert or how much a vehicle carries, our units and weights explainer covers it with a conversion table.

Step 3: Understand how coal pricing works

Coal is a market-linked commodity: rates change with mine prices, transport cost and season. That is why genuine depots quote on enquiry instead of printing a rate that would be wrong within weeks. A price quote is fast — message or call with three things: product, quantity, and delivery location. Location matters because delivery cost depends on distance and vehicle size.

Two buying tips from 35+ years in this trade: compare quotes on the same product and grade (a low quote for a different grade is not a saving), and for recurring needs — restaurants, kilns, factories — ask about repeat supply rather than pricing each order from scratch.

Step 4: Inspect before you buy (or buy on trust you can verify)

If it is your first order, visit the depot and look at the stock. Any supplier confident in their material will welcome it. Our yard at NH 37, Lalmati is open all 7 days, 8 AM to 8 PM — you can see hard coke grades, charcoal and steam coal side by side, compare lump sizes and pick up directly at no extra charge.
