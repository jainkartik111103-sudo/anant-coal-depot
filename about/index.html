About Anant Coal Depot

Some businesses are built on marketing. Ours is built on thirty-five years of the same family weighing coal honestly at the same depot on NH 37. This page is the plain record of who we are, what we stock, and why buyers across Assam and Northeast India have kept coming back since 1990.

Quick answer

Anant Coal Depot is a family-run coal supply business at NH 37, Lalmati, Near Nidhi Bhawan, Guwahati, Assam 781029, established in 1990 by Tarachand Jain and today run by Tarachand Jain and Anant Jain. It stocks hard coke (chota and bada grades), hard coke dust, wood charcoal, steam coal, earthing coal and earthing salt, supplying households, restaurants, brick kilns, foundries, tea gardens and contractors in bulk and retail across Guwahati, Assam, Northeast India and pan India. The story, without embellishment

Tarachand Jain established the depot at Lalmati in 1990, when NH 37 carried a fraction of today's traffic and coal was weighed on the same principle it is weighed on now: what the invoice says is what the vehicle carries. The business has stayed in the family since — today Tarachand Jain and Anant Jain run it together, and the buyer on the phone talks to a proprietor, not a call centre.

Over three and a half decades the depot grew from serving Guwahati households and bhattis into a supply point for brick kilns, foundries, rolling mills and aluminium utensil industries, hotels, restaurants and dhabas, tea gardens, traders and electrical contractors — with truck loads leaving NH 37 for every district of Assam, all Northeast states and pan India.

How we work

  • Inspect before you buy. The yard is open every day; every grade we sell sits in the open for you to judge.
  • No fixed minimum order. A household's few bags and a kiln's truck load get the same weighment and the same honesty.
  • Market-linked, quoted straight. Coal rates move; we quote today's rate for your product, quantity and destination in one call, delivery cost included.
  • GST billing. Registered businesses get proper invoices for input credit.
  • Knowledge shared freely. The Coal Knowledge Centre writes down what 35 years across a depot counter has taught us — useful whether you buy from us or not.
